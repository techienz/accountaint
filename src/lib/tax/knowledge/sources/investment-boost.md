# Investment Boost (Budget 2025)

A new tax incentive introduced in Budget 2025. Businesses can claim **20% of the cost of a qualifying new asset as an immediate deduction** in the year the asset is acquired or finished construction, then claim normal depreciation on the remaining 80%.

## Effective date

Applies to qualifying assets **acquired or finished construction on or after 22 May 2025**. Assets acquired before this date are not eligible.

## Eligible assets

- Most depreciable business assets: machinery, equipment, work vehicles, computer hardware, etc.
- **New commercial and industrial buildings** are eligible (even though buildings of this kind are not depreciable for income tax) — the 20% deduction still applies.
- Assets must be **new**, or **new to New Zealand** (e.g. imported second-hand assets that have never been used in NZ).

## Excluded

- **Residential buildings** are not eligible.
- Assets that have previously been used in New Zealand (i.e. genuinely second-hand within NZ).
- Assets held as trading stock (the rule covers business-use depreciable assets).

## No minimum value

There is **no minimum cost** for Investment Boost. The 20% applies even on small new assets. Note that the **$1,000 low-value asset write-off** is a separate rule — if an asset costs $1,000 or less you can already fully expense it under the low-value rule, so Investment Boost is most relevant for assets costing more than $1,000 that are otherwise depreciated.

## How the calculation works

Example: a new $50,000 piece of machinery acquired 1 August 2025.

- Investment Boost deduction (year 1): 20% × $50,000 = **$10,000** immediate expense.
- Depreciation base going forward: $50,000 − $10,000 = **$40,000**, depreciated using the normal IRD rate for that asset class.

## IR10 reporting

From the **2026 tax year** onwards, the Financial Statements Summary (IR10) includes a new line: **Total value of assets for which Investment Boost is being claimed**. Businesses must report the gross asset cost (not the deduction amount) for the year.
